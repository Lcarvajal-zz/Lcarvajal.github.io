Work achievements by Lukas


Founding Apps by Lukas

During college, I really wanted to have financial independence so I could do whatever I wanted. So I set a goal of earning $10,000 over the summer.

I first tried building personal websites for people, but I made little money from it since finding clients at a college was pretty tough. I failed that summer. However, I saw mobile apps were in high demand, so I went all-in improving my app development skills.

After about six months, I built a portfolio including a merge-text app, a location-based reminder app, and an app that bypassed newspaper article paywalls. That caught the attention of a professor of mine, who connected me with someone trying to build an iOS app for their startup.

That startup ended up contracting me to build their iOS, Android, and web apps. That quickly turned into me leading small teams to build apps for different clients. I made well over $10,000 the following summer, graduated early, and moved to Chicago to build my business.

Co-founding Tip Yourself

My passion for improving education stems from it being a major factor in economic empowerment. So when Tip Yourself hired Apps by Lukas to develop their iOS app, I got really excited about building a community of people motivating each other to save money.

During my first three months working with them, I revamped the entire app and designed a new bank sign up flow that increased their users from 300 to over 30,000. When I then solved an authentication problem an experienced consultant couldn't figure out, Tip Yourself asked me to join as co-founder and CTO.

It was a wild ride where we almost got into Tech Stars and Y Combinator, became a Top 20 Finance app on the App Store, and eventually got aquired by Earnin.

To write software that managed millions of dollars of people's money when I was 21 built a lot of confidence in my ability to lead larger and more complex projects.

Product Management at Mimo

I joined Mimo because I felt I had some experience building businesses and coding, but not really any industry experience in my biggest passion, education.

My first project was launching a course for Kotlin, but after a few weeks I jumped on opportunities to lead cross-functional teams for the first few iterations of challenges, improving our internal tools, and building our content management lifecycle.

My proudest achievements are my initiatives that shifted our company from a bunch of individuals focused on different goals, to teams of people making progress on a few important company goals.

3x Hackathon Winner

I love hackathons because I love the feeling of building something useful in a couple of days.

In 2015, my team won the Lacuna Open Build Hackathon for building a location-based social media app that encourage people to interact with each other in large office spaces.

In 2018, my team won the Mimo Company Hackathon for building a practice session generator that invented practice questions and interactions on its own. The proof-of-concept lead to a practice generation feature that saves course creators a few hours of work each week.

In 2019, my team won the Mimo Company Hackathon for building a one-click publish and one-click preview feature, which saved hours of work each week for course creators and developers.

The German Ecuadorian

My parents come from Ecuador and Germany, and decided to raise me in the US. While they raised me, they pushed me to become a good piano player and a good soccer player. Building those skills over several years taught me how to develop skills that take time and connect with all kinds of different people.

How I was raised shaped two beliefs in how I approach life. First, anyone can learn pretty much any thing. The factors that stop someone from learning are economic, motivational, opportunities to develop a skill, and knowledge gaps. Those things can be hard to fix, but are addressable with great teachers empowered by good policy and great educational products.

Second, everyone has a reason for doing things. The main reason is the environment they grew up in. If you listen to where a person comes from, you can start a conversation on where they should go. That's the best way to connect with someone, but like any other skill understanding takes time to develop.

When I explore the world and I can't speak the language, I fall back on my skills to build relationships. Playing a pick up game of soccer in China, playing a song from Mozart in Brazil, or cooking a Georgian dish in Georgia, are things that make a foreigner feel less foreign.

A great education is what shaped me into the person I am today. By dedicating my life to improving educationg, I'm aiming to create opportunities and build common ground for millions of others.

- Lukas Carvajal
